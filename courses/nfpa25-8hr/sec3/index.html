Section 5.3Inspection Looks · Testing Operates

What §5.3 Testing Covers

Sprinklers
Age-tested in a lab — proves the heat element still operates.
Waterflow Alarms
Tripped and confirmed to sound — mechanical and electric.
Antifreeze
Sampled and measured — proves freeze protection holds.
🔬Each test has a defined frequency or trigger age — and a defined action when it fails.

Section 5.3.1.2You Don't Pull Every Head

Representative Sample Testing

▸§5.3.1.2 — minimum sample = not less than 4 sprinklers, or 1% of the sprinklers per sample area, whichever is greater.
▸Small area → 4 sprinklers. Large area → 1% (more than 4).
▸The sample goes to a recognized testing laboratory for operation & response.
🧪The sample represents the whole area it was drawn from — which matters on the next slide.

Section 5.3.1.3One Failure Condemns the Batch

When a Sample Fails

▸§5.3.1.3 — if even one sprinkler in a representative sample fails, all sprinklers within the area represented by that sample must be replaced.
▸Not just the one that failed — the entire represented area.
Why
If one sprinkler of that age and environment has degraded, the others probably have too. One fails → they all get replaced.
⚠️This is why sampling is taken seriously — one failure = replace the whole area.

Sections 5.3.3.1–2Two Types · Two Frequencies

Waterflow Alarm Testing Frequencies

Quarterly
Mechanical waterflow alarm devices — including water motor gongs (§5.3.3.1).
Semiannually
Vane-type and pressure switch-type waterflow alarm devices (§5.3.3.2).
🔔Don't flip them: the mechanical water motor gong is quarterly — the more frequent one.

Section 5.3.3.3Frequency Is One Thing · Method Is Another

How You Test Waterflow Devices

▸§5.3.3.3 — on a wet pipe system, test a pressure switch-type device by opening the inspector's test connection.
▸The ITC simulates the flow of a single sprinkler at the most remote point — the true functional test.
Cold-Weather Allowance — §5.3.3.3.1
The bypass connection is permitted where freezing weather prohibits using the inspector's test connection. But the default answer is: open the ITC.

Section 5.3.3.5Never Quietly Leave Protection Off

Fire Pump During Testing

▸§5.3.3.5 — a fire pump shall not be taken out of service during testing unless:
▸① it is constantly attended by qualified personnel, OR
▸② all Chapter 15 impairment procedures are followed (tag + notifications).
🛡️Mirrors the water-supply rule from Section 1 — protection stays on, attended or formally impaired.

Section 5.3.4.3.1The Rules Tightened

Antifreeze Systems — What's Allowed

CPVC → Glycerine ONLY
§5.3.4.3.1 — listed CPVC sprinkler pipe & fittings shall be protected from freezing with glycerine only.
Prohibited in CPVC
§5.3.4.3.1.1 — diethylene, ethylene, and propylene glycols are prohibited — they can attack the plastic.
🧴"Only antifreeze permitted in CPVC?" → glycerine.

Section 5.3.4Verify Before Winter Arrives

Antifreeze Testing

▸§5.3.4 — antifreeze solutions tested annually, before the onset of freezing weather.
▸Determine the type of antifreeze.
▸Sample top and bottom of the system — concentration can stratify.
▸Measure specific gravity to confirm freeze protection is adequate.
❄️Annually, before freezing weather — verify protection before the pipe can freeze.

After You Find a Problem

▸An alarm that won't operate is a deficiency — possibly critical, possibly an impairment.
▸Document the result, date & code section (§4.3), and notify the owner in writing.
