Chapter 5Mostly Annual · One Exception

Inspection Frequencies

Annual
Sprinklers (from floor level, §5.2.1.1), pipe & fittings, hangers & braces, and signs.
Quarterly
Waterflow alarm devices and supervisory signal initiating devices (§5.2.4) — the item people miss.
📅Rule of thumb: Chapter 5 is annual — except waterflow & supervisory devices are quarterly.

Section 5.2.1.1The Floor-Level Look

Inspecting Sprinklers — The Basics

▸§5.2.1.1 — sprinklers inspected annually, from the floor level. No ladder required — a visual check from the ground, looking up.
Exception — §5.2.1.1.3
Sprinklers in concealed spaces (e.g. above a suspended ceiling) are NOT required to be inspected. You don't lift ceiling tiles. Accessible-space sprinklers still get the floor-level look.
👀Annual, from the floor — and concealed heads are exempt.

Section 5.2.1.1.1Replace — Not Clean, Not Note

Six Conditions That Require Replacement

1Leakage
2Corrosion affecting performance
3Physical damage
4Loss of fluid in the glass bulb
5Loading — dust, grease, or debris buildup
6Paint not applied by the manufacturer
🎨Any field-applied paint — even light overspray — requires replacement. It insulates the heat element.

Section 5.2.1Three Details That Trip People Up

Orientation, Escutcheons & Loading

Orientation
Wrong position for its type (pendent installed upright) won't develop the designed pattern — a finding.
Escutcheons
Matter most on concealed/recessed heads. Missing on a standard pendent = not a replacement condition.
Loading
Judged by whether buildup affects sensitivity/pattern. Light dust ≠ loading.
🔎Distinguish true loading from light surface dust — only real buildup requires action.

Section 5.2.1.2Numbers That Show Up on the Quiz

Deflector Clearance to Storage

18 in. (457 mm)
Standard sprinklers (§5.2.1.2.1) — minimum clearance from deflector to top of storage. Gives the spray room to develop.
36 in. (914 mm)
Rubber tire storage (§5.2.1.2.4) and special sprinklers — tires burn hot and fast.
📏18 inches standard · 36 inches for rubber tires and special sprinklers.

Section 5.2.1.4Three Checks During the Annual Inspection

The Spare Sprinkler Cabinet

1
Correct number and type of spare sprinklers present
2
A sprinkler wrench for each type installed
3
A posted list of the spare sprinklers
🗄️Stock tiers: under 300 → 6 · 300–1,000 → 12 · over 1,000 → 24 (revisited in Section 4).

Section 5.2.2The Pipe Carries Water — Not the Trades

Inspecting Pipe & Fittings

▸§5.2.2 — inspect for leaks, corrosion, mechanical damage, and proper alignment.
▸§5.2.2.2 — PROHIBITED: materials resting on the pipe, or items hung from the pipe.
No External Loads
No conduit, cables, ductwork, or anyone else's equipment strapped to your sprinkler pipe. It's engineered for water and its own weight — not to be a support.
🚫Loaded pipe = a code violation you correct and document.

Section 5.2.3What Holds the System Up

Hangers, Braces & Supports

▸§5.2.3 — verify hangers, braces, and supports are in place, secure, and free of corrosion or damage.
▸§5.2.3.2 — a hanger found damaged, loose, or unattached shall be replaced or refastened.
▸There's no "monitor it" option — the required action is immediate correction.
🔩A system on failed supports is a static and a seismic failure waiting to happen.

Section 5.2.4The Quarterly Exception

Waterflow & Supervisory Devices

▸§5.2.4 — waterflow alarm devices and supervisory signal initiating devices are inspected QUARTERLY — four times a year.
▸These tell someone the system activated, or that a valve was tampered with — so the code watches them closely.
🔔If you remember one non-annual frequency from Chapter 5: waterflow & supervisory = quarterly.

Section 5.2.5Small Placards, Big Information

Required Signs

▸The hydraulic design information sign at the riser gives the design basis: density, design area, flow & pressure.
▸§5.2.5.1 — if it's missing or illegible, it shall be replaced. Mandatory — no grace period.
▸§5.2.5.2 — a pipe-schedule system gets a sign reading "Pipe Schedule System."
🪧These placards carry the information the whole ITM history depends on.

FieldInspection Is What You Do With What You Find

After You Find a Problem

▸Document the deficiency: what it is, where it is, and the code section.
▸Notify the owner — the record proves the condition was found and communicated.
▸Escalate critical deficiencies and impairments per the rules from Section 1 (Chapter 15).
