Section 13.3.2Control Valves · A Closed Valve = No Water

Control Valves — Inspection Frequencies

Weekly
§13.3.2.1 · DEFAULT
All control valves — the baseline. Sealed valves live here.
Monthly
§13.3.2.1.1
Valves secured with locks or supervised per an applicable NFPA standard.
Quarterly
§13.3.2.1.2
Valves that are electrically supervised.
Inspection Checklist — §13.3.2.2
① Normal open/closed position · ② sealed, locked, or supervised · ③ accessible · ④ PIVs have correct wrenches · ⑤ free from external leaks · ⑥ proper identification signage.

Section 13.3.3Inspecting Says It Looks Right · Operating Says It Works

Control Valves — Annual Operation

▸§13.3.3.1 — each control valve operated annually through its full range and returned to normal.
▸§13.3.3.2 — PIVs: open until spring or torsion is felt in the rod — proves it's fully open.
▸§13.3.3.3 — PIVs and OS&Y valves: back off one-quarter turn from fully open to prevent jamming.
▸§13.3.3.5.1 — valve supervisory switches tested semiannually; signal trips within two handwheel turns or one-fifth of stem travel.
🔄Full range means fully closed and back fully open — then verify the indicator.

Section 13.2.3Checking the Water Supply Itself

The Main Drain Test

▸§13.2.3 — conducted annually at each water-supply lead-in to detect a change in the water supply condition.
▸§13.2.3.2 — QUARTERLY on at least one system where the sole supply is through a backflow preventer and/or PRV.
▸§13.2.3.3 — a 10% reduction in full-flow pressure vs. the original acceptance test must be investigated and corrected.
New in 2020 — §13.3.3.4
Any time a control valve is closed and reopened, run a valve status test at the riser — proves the supply is fully restored.
📉10% is the trigger — those devices can fail partially closed, and the main drain is how you catch it.

Section 13.2Two Quick Components

Gauges & Waterflow Alarm Devices

Gauges §13.2.5
Replaced every 5 years, OR tested every 5 years against a calibrated gauge (§13.2.5.2).
▸ Not accurate within 3% of full scale → recalibrate or replace (§13.2.5.3)
▸ Inspected monthly for operability (§13.2.5.1.1)
Waterflow Alarms §13.2.4
Mechanical (water motor gong) tested quarterly; vane-type & pressure-switch tested semiannually.
▸ On dry / preaction / deluge, test the alarm via the bypass connection — no trip needed
⏱️Gauges = 5 years / 3% of full scale. Waterflow: mechanical quarterly, electric semiannual.

Sections 13.4.1–13.4.2The Valve Family Begins

Alarm Valves & Check Valves

Alarm Valves §13.4.1
Externally inspected quarterly (§13.4.1.1): gauges normal, no damage, valves in position, retard chamber / alarm drains not leaking.
▸ Interior every 5 years — strainers, filters, restriction orifices (§13.4.1.2)
Check Valves §13.4.2
Internally inspected every 5 years (§13.4.2.1) — verify they move freely and seat correctly.
▸ Five years is the recurring interval for the internals of most of these devices
🔎Alarm valve exterior = quarterly; interiors of alarm & check valves = every 5 years.

Section 13.4.5Air Over Water · Watch the Numbers

Dry Pipe Valves — Inspection & Routine Checks

▸§13.4.5.1.2 — exterior inspected monthly (no damage, trim valves in position, intermediate chamber not leaking).
▸§13.4.5.1.3 — interior inspected annually, when the trip test is performed.
▸§13.4.5.2.1 — priming water level tested quarterly.
▸§13.4.5.2.4 — quick-opening devices tested quarterly.
▸§13.4.5.2.6 — low air pressure alarm tested annually.
⚠️2020 numbering: 13.4.5 = dry, 13.4.3 = preaction, 13.4.4 = deluge. Don't mix them up.

Section 13.4.5.2.2Proving the Valve Actually Trips

Dry Pipe Valves — Trip Tests

Annual Trip §13.4.5.2.2
Every year, in warm weather, with the control valve partially open (§13.4.5.2.2.3).
Full-Flow Trip §13.4.5.2.2.2
Every 3 years — and whenever the system is altered — control valve fully open, QOD in service.
▸Air / nitrogen restored within 30 minutes after refill (§13.4.5.2.2.4).
▸Gas leakage test every 3 years — 40 psi for 2 hours, ≤3 psi loss (§13.4.5.2.9).
▸Record trip pressure & water delivery time; tag results on the valve (§13.4.5.2.5).

Sections 13.4.3 / 13.4.4Two Sections · Two Different Rules

Preaction & Deluge Valves

Preaction §13.4.3
Trip tested annually with the control valve partially open (§13.4.3.2.3).
▸ Full-flow trip every 3 years, control valve fully open (§13.4.3.2.2)
▸ Low air alarm quarterly
Deluge §13.4.4
Trip tested annually at FULL FLOW in warm weather (§13.4.4.2.3).
▸ Open-headed by design — full flow every year
▸ No-discharge trip allowed where water would damage equipment
📌Preaction full-flow = every 3 years. Deluge full-flow = every year. Different animals.

Section 13.7Keep Fire Water Out of the Potable Supply

Backflow Prevention Assemblies

▸§13.7.1.1 — RPZ assemblies inspected weekly to verify the differential-sensing relief port is not continuously discharging.
▸§13.7.1.3 — internal inspection every 5 years.
▸§13.7.2.1 — annual forward-flow test at system demand (exercise the assembly).
▸§13.7.2.2 — where hydrants or inside hose stations are downstream, include hose stream demand.
▸§13.7.2.1.2 — not required where the annual fire pump test already flows through the device.
↔️Backflow = §13.7 (13.6 is hose valves). Forward-flow test annually at system demand.

Section 13.8How the Fire Department Pumps In

Fire Department Connections — Quarterly

✓Visible and accessible
✓Couplings/swivels undamaged, rotate smoothly
✓Plugs/caps in place and undamaged
✓Gaskets in place
✓Identification signs in place
✓Check valve not leaking

Section 13.3.1A Closed Valve Nobody Can Identify Is a Hazard

Valve Signage & Securing

▸§13.3.1 — every control valve identified with a sign stating what it controls.
▸§13.3.1.1 — multi-valve systems: each valve's sign references the existence and location of the others.
▸§13.3.1.3 — normally open valves secured with a seal or lock, or electrically supervised.
▸§13.3.1.4 — normally closed valves secured by seal or electrically supervised.
▸Dry/preaction risers: sign stating the number and location of auxiliary drains.
🏷️Signs and seals are cheap — a mystery valve found closed after a fire is not.

Section 5.4Sprinkler Maintenance & the Spare Cabinet

Replace Right — Stock the Cabinet

▸§5.4.1.1 — a sprinkler removed for any reason shall not be reinstalled. Out means done.
▸§5.4.1.2 — replacements match: style, orifice/K-factor, temperature rating, coating, deflector type.
▸§5.4.1.7 — sprinklers over commercial cooking equipment replaced annually (grease loading).
Spare Sprinkler Cabinet — §5.4.1.5
Minimum 6 spares. Tiers (§5.4.1.5.4): under 300 heads → ≥6 · 300–1,000 → ≥12 · over 1,000 → ≥24. Plus one wrench per sprinkler type + a posted spare list.
🗄️Spare stock: 6 · 12 · 24 — memorize the tiers.
