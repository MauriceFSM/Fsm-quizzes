NFPA 13 · 13R · 13D

The Designer's Map — pick your track, flip without traps

🏰 NFPA 13 — protects the whole castle

Every room, every closet, every attic. Mission: save the people and the building. Big water, heads everywhere, full paperwork.

🏢 NFPA 13R — protects everyone's way out

Low-rise apartments & small hotels (≤4 stories, ≤60 ft). Thinks like 13D inside each unit — skip the tiny baths and closets — but like 13 for the shared bones: garages, attics, stairwells and corridors all get heads, because many families' escape rides on them. 4-head design area, 30 minutes of water.

🚪 NFPA 13D — protects one family's path out

One job: give the family about 10 minutes of escape time from the living areas. It doesn't care if the garage or attic burns.

Memory hook: 13 protects the property · 13R protects the shared way out · 13D protects one family's way out.
More people sharing the building → fewer things you're allowed to skip.

Step 0 — Which book am I in?

What is the building?

NFPA 13D allowed (per IBC 903.3.1.3). Go to the 13D Track tab. ⚠️ But: 13D earns zero IBC height/area increases — if the design counts on those, you're back in NFPA 13.
NFPA 13R allowed (per IBC 903.3.1.2) — the middle sibling, up to 4 stories / 60 ft. Go to the 13R Track tab. It thinks like 13D inside the units but like 13 for the shared bones (garages, attics, stairs, corridors). ⚠️ Still earns zero IBC height/area increases.
Full NFPA 13 required (per IBC 903.3.1.1). Go to the 13 Track tab. Only NFPA 13 unlocks IBC height/area increases (IBC Tables 504.3, 504.4, 506.2).

The #1 13R trap: designers carry the 13D omit list into a 13R job and leave garages, attics, stairwells and corridors bare. In 13D those are optional because one family is escaping their own house. In 13R, other people's escape runs through those shared spaces — so they get heads (per NFPA 13R §7.3, §7.4, §6.6.9). Inside the unit, 13R and 13D skip the same tiny baths and closets; outside the unit, 13R behaves like 13.
